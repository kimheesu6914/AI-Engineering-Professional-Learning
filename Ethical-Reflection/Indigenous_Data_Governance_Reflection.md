# Goal 1: Indigenous Data Sovereignty & Professional Accountability

As an aspiring AI Engineer, I have realized that technical excellence must be supported by ethical data governance. This reflection focuses on how Indigenous Data Sovereignty (IDS) intersects with AI development.

## Core Principles in Practice
* **Free, Prior and Informed Consent (FPIC)**: Before using any dataset, it is crucial to verify if the community provided consent voluntarily and with full knowledge of how their data would be utilized in AI models.
* **Data Sovereignty**: Recognising that Indigenous communities have the right to govern the collection and application of data that affects them.

## Technical Integration
When I performed the data preprocessing in **Goal 4**, I focused on accuracy. However, looking through the lens of **Goal 1**, I now understand that:
1. **Context Matters**: Data is not just 'raw material'; it carries cultural and historical weight.
2. **Bias Prevention**: Ethical data cleaning involves ensuring that the dataset does not marginalize or misrepresent Indigenous perspectives.

## Professional Growth
Completing the Indigenous Professional Capability module has shifted my identity from a 'code-centric' developer to a 'responsibility-centric' AI Engineer. I will commit to checking data provenance and respecting community rights in my future career.
